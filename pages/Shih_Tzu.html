The Shih Tzu ( simplified Chinese : 狮子狗 ; traditional Chinese : 獅子狗 ; pinyin : Shīzi Gǒu ; Wade–Giles : Shih-tzu Kou ; literally: "Lion Dog"), ʂʐ dz   (i)     [ "sher-dz" ] (with "sher" as in "sherbet" and "dz" as in "adz") [1] , is a dog breed which originated in China . The name is both singular and plural. The spelling "Shih Tzu", most commonly used for the breed, is according to the Wade-Giles system of romanization. The Shih Tzu is reported to be the oldest and smallest of the Tibetan holy dogs, [ citation needed ] its vaguely lion -like look being associated with the Tibetan Snowlion . [ citation needed ] The Shih Tzu therefore also has the nickname "Tibetan temple dog". It is also often known as the "Xi Shi quan" (西施犬), based on the name of Xi Shi , regarded as the most beautiful woman of ancient China [2] , and, less often, the Chrysanthemum Dog, because its face looks very much like the flower, and the Chinese or Tibetan Lion Dog.

The Shih Tzu or Shih Tsu (pronounced Shid Zoo in singular and plural) (獅子狗 pinyin : Shīzi Gǒu, Wade-Giles : Shih-tzu Kou) is a dog breed which originated in Tibet . The spelling "Shih Tzu", most commonly used for the breed, is according to the Wade-Giles system of romanization. The Chinese pronunciation of this name is approximately like the "sher" of "sherbet" followed immediately by the "dds" of "adds". The Shih Tzu is reported to be the oldest and smallest of the Tibetan holy dogs, its lion -like look being associated with the Snowlion . It is now often called by a homonymic name, "xi shi quan" (西施犬), based on the name of Xi Shi , regarded as the most beautiful woman of ancient China.
